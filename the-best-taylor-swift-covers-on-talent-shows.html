The Best Taylor Swift Covers on Talent Shows

TALENTRECAP.COM Entertainment Trending

NBC

Reading Time: 3 minutes

With Taylor Swift being an acclaimed superstar, it’s definitely not surprising to hear a rendition of her song during a competition series. Certainly, nothing could beat the original. Yet, it’s also worth giving a watch to beautiful and unique renditions.

We compiled some of the best Taylor Swift music covers to ever exist from televised talent shows. Let’s take look at them.

The Best Taylor Swift Covers on Talent Shows

10. Hannah Eyre, The Voice USA 2017

Singing Swift’s 2014 hit “Blank Space,” Hannah Eyre earned three-chair turns. Certainly, her clean voice and unique touch to the song have made her rendition a button magnet.

9. Daniel Duke, The Voice UK 2015

Incorporating his distinct acoustic-folk style to Swift’s “Shake It Off,” Daniel Duke proved that he was deserving to be a part of Team Tom. His rendition was chill yet energizing, a good way to hype up the audience. Hence, while he didn’t proceed to the live shows, Duke has attracted fans due to this awesome rendition.

8. Olivia, The Voice van Vlaanderen 2016

Seemingly holding back at first, Olivia Zingt belted the pop star’s “Blank Space” in an attempt to get through the Blind Auditions. Yet, even when nervous, her crisp vocals weren’t concealed. Such effortless style and youthful voice made her proceed to the competition.

7. Victoria Stoichkova, The Voice Bulgaria 2018

“Look What You Made Me Do” is already an eerie song, but Victoria Stoichkova has convinced The Voice Bulgaria fans that it could be more bizarre. Stoichkova surprised the audience when she unleashed the heavy metal aspect of her vocals, making Swift’s 2017 hit her own.

6. Jacob Maxwell, The Voice USA 2019

Belting “Delicate” during his audition, Jacob Maxwell successfully showed off his awesome vocals. With this performance, both Kelly Clarkson and John Legend turned their chairs for the singer.

5. Alex & Sierra, The X Factor USA 2013

Overwhelmed by the disconcerting comments of the judges, Sierra needed to make sure that her next performance with Alex would be impeccable. The two have chosen “I Knew You Were Trouble,” blending their own approach to the song. Undoubtedly, this has been one of the most exceptional versions of Swift’s 2012 music hit.

4. Elise Baker, The Voice Australia 2014

Swaying the coaches with her angelic voice, Elise Baker performed “Safe and Sound” during the Blind Auditions. While Baker didn’t really change the tune of the song, she’s effortlessly showcased how emotive her voice could be.

3. Ivonne Acero, The Voice USA 2015

Ivonne Acero first graced The Voice USA stage back in his Blind Auditions in 2015. She sang Swift’s famous hit “Style” and made it her own, ultimately attracting Pharrell Williams and Gwen Stefani to turn for her. This rendition is undeniably one-of-a-kind.

2. Caro Trischler, The Voice Germany 2013

If we are to choose one word to describe Caro Trischler’s audition performance, that would be “suave.” She has managed to convert “I Knew You Were Trouble” into a different song, showcasing her capability as an inventive musician.

1. Union J, The X Factor UK 2014

During the Live Shows, Union J performed Swift’s “Love Story,” Union J’s farewell indeed became memorable. With this performance, we’re definitely more convinced that the 2008 music smash could be an ultimate boy band song.
