Robin Thicke, Jenny McCarthy Talk About Departing 'The Masked Singer'

The anticipation is building as Fox’s beloved reality singing competition, The Masked Singer, gears up for its milestone Season 10. What’s even more promising is the dedication of two of its beloved panelists, Robin Thicke and Jenny McCarthy. Along with host Nick Cannon, they discussed their thoughts on the milestone season and when they might eventually step away from their roles on the show.

Robin Thicke, Jenny McCarthy Both Don’t Plan To Leave The Show Anytime Soon

As a seasoned panelist on the show, McCarthy exuded enthusiasm when asked about her future on The Masked Singer.

“I have 30 more seasons in me,” she said.

Her commitment to the show is unwavering, and she couldn’t be more thrilled to be a part of the 10th season, describing it as a dream come true.

Thicke, a prominent panelist for several seasons, reflected on the lightning-fast journey he has experienced on the show. Thicke emphasized how being on the show has been one of the most enjoyable experiences of his career, relishing the opportunity to watch extraordinary performances, experience the show’s madness, and interact with the enthusiastic audience.

“I’m down! Yeah, I’m down for 25 seasons,” Thicke said.

Cannon, who has been the show’s charismatic host since its debut in 2019, couldn’t hide his excitement at the show’s success. Cannon proudly wears the show’s unconventional and zany nature as a badge of honor. He recalled how, prior to joining The Masked Singer, he had been primarily focused on behind-the-scenes work. When the opportunity came to host a show that was both fun and unique, he leaped at it.

The Masked Singer has managed to capture the hearts of viewers and retain its popularity due to its continuous improvement and its place as a cultural touchstone. Cannon believes the show has become a cultural staple, with fans eagerly awaiting each season’s roster of hidden celebrities.

How To Watch The Masked Singer Season 10

As the highly anticipated Season 10 of The Masked Singer kicks off, fans are eagerly preparing to tune in and witness the magic unfold. Here’s all the information you need to ensure you catch the rest of season 10.

The new season of The Masked Singer aired its first episode on September 10, 2023, at 8:00 p.m. ET, exclusively on FOX. For those who prefer streaming, there are options available to watch the premiere’s replay. You can live stream The Masked Singer Season 10 Premiere on FuboTV, and they even offer a free trial to get you started.

It’s worth noting that while the season officially began on September 10, the competition itself will commence on September 27. This season introduces a fresh format, dividing the contestants into three groups of singers, leading to exciting Battle Royale semifinal episodes for each group. With a total of 16 masked contestants, including intriguing characters like Donut, Hawk, Hibiscus, and S’More, viewers are in for a thrilling ride.
